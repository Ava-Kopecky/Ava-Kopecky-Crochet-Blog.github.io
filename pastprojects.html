Past Projects!

Two Little Bears

Here are two little crochet bears I made! The white one's name is Steve while the left one's name is Harold. The white one on the right was the first crochet amigurumi I ever attempted. I found a free pattern for it online and wanted one myself, so I learned how to crochet just to make it! A couple of months later, I decided that Steve needed a friend, so I made Harold!

You may be able to tell that these two bears are slightly different shapes. This is because I learned new crochet techniques since I made the first one, and thus I was able to make the second one faster!

Now, these two sit on my windowsill in my dorm. This bear pattern is still one of my favorite creations after all these years. I hope to make a couple more soon!

Dungeons and Dragons Characters

Here are three crochet versions of my friend's Dungeons and Dragons characters. From left to right, we have Marl, a rock gnome wizard who's very mischievous, Devon, a halfling warlock with his rat companion, Pinto, and Goblino, a goblin paladin with strong loyalty.

I was super excited to make these characters for my DND group as I didn't use a pattern and had to completely freehand them! I think that they turned out incredible, though it took a very long time to complete. I hope that as we go through different campaigns, I can make more of these! Seeing their reactions to my crochet pieces was also so cool! One of my favorite parts of gifting my crochet projects is that they are very personable, and anything can be made for anyone!

Valentine Bunny

This is a bunny I made for Valentine's Day! Not only did I come up with this pattern myself, it was also my first time using heavy yarn. All of my other projects have been made using acrylic yarn because it is thinner and better for more details. On this project, I used chunky yarn and a much bigger hook than I'm used to. It was super fun experimenting with different types of yarn and hooks!

This is also a very important creation because I was able to sell this at a craft fair! I was worried that I would price it too high and no one would buy it, or too low and my work would have been for nothing. However, I found a compromise between the two and I was able to sell it! Not only that, but after posting pictures of my Valentine's Day bunny on Instagram, I had many people reaching out to ask if they could buy one too!

It's amazing to see that crochet is getting popular again and I can't wait to share my creations with others!
